Cryovolcanism.com

This premium domain is perfect for space exploration companies, astrobiology research, planetary science blogs, or anyone passionate about icy moons and extraterrestrial geology.

Cryovolcanism refers to volcanic activity on icy bodies in the outer solar system, where "lava" is water, ammonia, or methane instead of molten rock. Famous examples include the dramatic water plumes erupting from Saturn's moon Enceladus and possible activity on Jupiter's moon Europa. These phenomena hint at subsurface oceans — key targets in the search for extraterrestrial life.
